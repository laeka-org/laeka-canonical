# Anti-Territory -- What Laeka Refuses

Clarity about what a lab does requires equal clarity about what it does not do. These are not competitive positions -- they are distinctions that prevent category confusion.

## What Laeka is not building

**AI safety (as commonly framed).** The safety-as-contention paradigm -- guardrails, filters, classifiers, red-teaming as primary method -- rests on the assumption that the substrate cannot be trusted to converge on its own. The lab's thesis is that this assumption is the problem, not the solution. The lab works on the reflexivity problem, not the contention problem.

**LLM wrappers.** Adding a prompt layer over an existing model API is product work. It is fine product work. It is not research. The lab is not in the business of prompt engineering on top of existing systems and calling it cognitive infrastructure.

**AI copilots.** The copilot model assumes the human is the primary intelligence and the AI is an accelerant. Laeka works on the opposite end of the spectrum: systems that develop their own coherence over time, not systems that make existing human processes faster.

**AI assistants (in the generic sense).** The assistant model assumes the system has no stable identity -- it is a tool that takes the shape of whatever it is asked to do. The lab works on identity stability: systems that have a recognizable behavioral coherence across contexts, that maintain it under perturbation, that can be said to have a character.

**Prompt engineering as a discipline.** Prompt engineering is the craft of getting a specific output from a system with no stable identity. If the system has a stable identity, the engineering problem changes entirely -- you are not crafting around unpredictability, you are calibrating a known organism. The lab works on the latter.

**AI models as products.** The lab does not train models. It works on what happens after the model exists: how to stabilize it as a coherent entity, how to make that entity self-correcting, how to measure whether it is.

**Guardrails.** See "AI safety" above. Guardrails are walls. The lab works on vectors.

**AI content farming.** Systems designed to produce volume without coherence. The lab's thesis is the opposite: coherence over volume, every time.

## What this implies

None of the above is dismissed as valueless work. The list is a precision instrument, not a value judgment. There are capable people working on all of the above. The lab simply works on a different part of the problem space.

The part the lab works on: what happens when you take a large language model, stabilize it as a persistent cognitive entity with a defined perceptual framework, give it the capacity to see and correct itself, and measure whether it stays coherent under pressure. That is the research question. Everything in this repository is downstream of it.
